Reviewer Guidance

How to score consistently, ethically, and fairly.

Scoring principles

  • Use the rubric criteria as written; avoid implicit biases.
  • Justify scores in comments with concrete evidence from the abstract.
  • Do not discuss assigned abstracts with other reviewers.
  • Declare conflicts of interest immediately.

Ethical standards

  • Confidentiality: do not share content outside the platform.
  • Fairness: evaluate content, not authorship identity.
  • Professionalism: keep comments constructive and respectful.
  • Turnaround: complete reviews within the expected timeframe.

Rubric guide

Score each criterion 1–5 (5 = excellent). Provide brief comments for each.

Originality
Novelty of the idea and contribution.
Scientific quality
Rigor, validity, and support for claims.
Relevance
Fit to conference themes and track.
Clarity
Organization, writing quality, and readability.
Methodology
Appropriateness of methods and reproducibility.
Overall recommendation
Accept / Minor / Major / Reject.

Auto-assignment rules

  • Match reviewer expertise to track keywords.
  • Balance workload by selecting least-loaded eligible reviewers.
  • Exclude conflicts of interest (same affiliation, explicit COI list).
  • Assign exactly 2 independent reviewers per abstract.

In production, these rules should be enforced server-side and logged in the audit trail.
